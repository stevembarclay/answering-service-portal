# Client Portal Guide

Your answering service has set up a portal so you can see all your calls in one place — in real time, from any device.

This guide walks you through everything: signing in for the first time, reading your messages, managing who's on call, and understanding your billing.

## 1. Signing In for the First Time

You'll receive an email from your answering service with a subject line like **"You've been invited"** or **"Set up your portal."**

1. **Click the link in the email.** It sets up your account and logs you in automatically.
   > The link expires after 24 hours. If it has expired, contact your answering service and ask them to resend the invite.

2. You'll land on the **Account Setup Wizard**. Walk through the steps to configure your:
   - Business name and time zone
   - Business hours and call types
   - Greeting script (what your answering service says when they pick up)
   - Who's on call (contacts and escalation steps)
   - Message delivery preferences

   Each step takes about a minute. You can skip sections and come back later — your progress is saved automatically.

3. After setup, you'll land on your **Dashboard** every time you log in.

**Signing in again later:**

Go to the portal URL your answering service provided. Enter your email address and choose:
- **Send me a login link** — receive a one-click email link (no password needed)
- **Sign in with password** — if you've set a password

## 2. Adding the Portal to Your Phone

Checking messages on your phone is easy. You can add the portal as an app so it looks and behaves like a native app:

**iPhone (Safari):**
1. Open the portal URL in Safari.
2. Tap the **Share** button (box with arrow).
3. Tap **Add to Home Screen**.
4. Tap **Add**.

**Android (Chrome):**
1. Open the portal URL in Chrome.
2. Tap the **three-dot menu**.
3. Tap **Add to Home Screen**.
4. Tap **Add**.

You'll get a home screen icon and a full-screen experience without the browser address bar.

## 3. Your Dashboard

The dashboard is your real-time overview of call activity. It refreshes automatically — no need to reload the page.

### Priority Calls

At the top of the dashboard is a feed of **unread urgent and priority calls**. These are calls your answering service flagged as needing immediate attention.

Each card shows:
- Caller name and number
- The call message
- How long ago the call came in

Click any card to open the full message detail.

### Call Volume Chart

A 7-day bar chart showing the number of calls per day. Use it to spot busy days and trends.

### Stats at a glance

| Metric | What it means |
|--------|--------------|
| Unread messages | Calls you haven't opened yet since your last login |
| Calls this week | Total calls in the last 7 days |
| Busiest hour | The hour of day with the most call volume (last 30 days) |

### Billing estimate

A running total of your estimated bill for the current billing period. Updated in real time as calls arrive.

## 4. Messages

The **Messages** page is your full call inbox. All calls your answering service takes on your behalf appear here, sorted newest first.

### Filtering and searching

| Filter | Shows |
|--------|-------|
| **All** | Every call |
| **Unread** | Calls not yet opened |
| **Priority** | Calls marked high priority |

Use the **search bar** to find calls by caller name, phone number, or message content.

### Understanding call cards

Each call in the list shows:
- **Caller name** (if provided by the answering service)
- **Phone number**
- **Call time and date**
- **Priority badge** — High / Medium / Low
- **Status badge** — Unread / Read
- **Call type** — e.g. Urgent, New Client, Appointment

### Real-time updates

New calls appear automatically at the top of the list as they come in — no refresh needed. You'll also see a brief notification in the corner of the screen when a new call arrives.

The **Messages** item in the sidebar shows a dot when you have unread messages.

## 5. Message Detail

Click any call to open the full detail view.

### What's shown

| Section | Contents |
|---------|----------|
| **Caller info** | Name, phone number, callback number |
| **Call info** | Date and time, duration, call type, direction |
| **Message** | The agent's written call note — what the caller said and what they need |
| **Voicemail recording** | Audio player (if the call was a voicemail and a recording was uploaded) |
| **Action history** | Log of any priority changes, status updates, or flags |

### Priority

The priority level is set automatically based on call type (urgent calls default to high priority), but you or your staff can adjust it:

- Click the **priority selector** and choose High, Medium, or Low
- The change is logged in the action history with your name and timestamp
- High-priority calls are highlighted in red throughout the portal

### Flagging for review

If something is wrong with a message — the answering service made an error, the message is incomplete, or the call was mishandled — click **Flag for QA Review** (the flag icon).

This marks the call for your answering service to review. Your answering service can see all flagged calls in their admin portal.

### Marking as read

Opening a message automatically marks it as read. Your unread count in the sidebar updates immediately.

## 6. On-Call Schedule

The **On-Call** section lets you manage who should be contacted after hours when an urgent message comes in.

Your answering service can see the current on-call contact and use it to route urgent calls appropriately.

### Contacts

First, add the people who take calls:

1. Go to **On-Call → Contacts → Add Contact**.
2. Enter: name, phone number, role (e.g. "Physician," "Office Manager"), and any notes.
3. Save.

Add all the people in your rotation — you can have as many as you need.

### Shifts

A **shift** defines when a group of contacts is on call.

1. Go to **On-Call → Shifts → Add Shift**.
2. Give the shift a name (e.g. "Weeknight," "Weekend").
3. Select which days of the week it applies.
4. Set the start and end time (in your business's time zone).
5. Add **escalation steps**: who to call first, how long to wait before moving to the next person.

**Example shift — "Weeknight":**
- Days: Monday–Friday
- Hours: 5:00 PM – 9:00 AM
- Escalation:
  1. Dr. Jones — wait 5 minutes
  2. Dr. Chen — wait 10 minutes
  3. Nurse Practitioner Kim — final step

Overnight shifts (e.g. 8 PM to 8 AM) are supported.

### Viewing who's on call right now

At the top of the **On-Call** page you'll see the **current active shift** and the first contact in the escalation. This is what your answering service sees when a call comes in.

If no shift is configured for the current time, the page shows "No active shift."

### Time zone

Your on-call schedule runs in your business's time zone. To change your time zone, go to **On-Call → Settings → Time Zone**.

## 7. Billing

The **Billing** section shows your current estimate and invoice history.

### Current estimate

The current estimate is a running calculation of what you owe for this billing period, based on your billing rules and call data received so far. It updates in real time as calls arrive.

The estimate shows:
- Each billing rule and its current amount (e.g. "Base monthly fee: $150.00", "Urgent calls (24 × $2.50): $60.00")
- A total

This is an **estimate** — your final invoice is generated by your answering service at the end of the billing period.

### Invoices

Past invoices appear in the **Invoices** tab. Each invoice shows:
- Billing period (start and end date)
- Line items (each billing rule and its amount)
- Total

You can download invoices as PDF from the invoice detail page.

## 8. Account Settings

### Updating your profile

Your login email is managed by your answering service. To change it, contact them directly.

### Changing your password

Go to your portal's login page and click **Forgot password**. An email will be sent with a reset link.

### Team members

If you have multiple staff members who need access (e.g. an office manager and a doctor), ask your answering service to add them as additional users on your account.

## 9. Getting Help

### In-portal assistant

Click the **chat icon** in the bottom corner of the dashboard for the AI account assistant. It can answer questions about your portal, help with setup, and explain your billing.

> **Note:** The chat assistant is available only if your answering service has configured AI support. If you do not see the chat icon, contact your answering service.

### Contact your answering service

For questions about specific calls, billing disputes, or service changes, contact your answering service directly using the contact information they provided when you were invited.

### Technical issues

If something isn't loading or you're experiencing a technical problem with the portal itself, contact your answering service and ask them to report it to their portal provider.
